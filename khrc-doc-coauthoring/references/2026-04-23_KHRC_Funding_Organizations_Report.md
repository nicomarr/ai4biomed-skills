# Funding Organizations for Kintampo Health Research Centre (KHRC) Researchers: A Comprehensive Guide to Global and African Grant Opportunities

## Introduction

The Kintampo Health Research Centre (KHRC) in Ghana operates across a broad portfolio of public health research areas — clinical trials, environmental health, family and maternal health, malaria and neglected tropical diseases (NTDs), health policy and program evaluation, and operational/implementation research. This report maps out more than 25 international, US government, European, and African regional funders whose grant mechanisms are open to African-based or sub-Saharan Africa-led institutions like KHRC. Each entry describes the funder, relevant grant mechanism(s), the research areas covered, whether the funder specifically supports African/LMIC-based researchers, and the direct URL to the funding page.

It is worth noting that the global aid landscape shifted significantly in 2025, with much of USAID's funding to Ghana and sub-Saharan Africa suspended or terminated under the Trump administration, reshaping which donors are realistically available to African researchers. KHRC should therefore prioritize funders with stable, African-facing mandates (EDCTP3, NIHR, Wellcome, SFA Foundation, Gates Foundation, IDRC, WHO/TDR, and continental African funders).

## 1. International Funders

### 1.1 Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation
The Gates Foundation is one of the largest private funders globally for malaria, NTDs, maternal/neonatal/child health and vaccines, with explicit strategies to fund African-led research. In 2022 it pledged US$140 million over four years specifically to support African institutions working on malaria and NTDs. The Foundation also operates the **Grand Challenges** family of programs — open calls that directly fund African investigators to tackle maternal/neonatal/child health, climate and health, malaria and other priorities.
- **Research areas covered:** Malaria, NTDs, maternal/neonatal/child health, vaccines, climate & health, implementation research.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — explicit African-led funding mechanism via Grand Challenges Africa.
- **URL:** https://gcgh.grandchallenges.org/ and https://www.gatesfoundation.org/about/committed-grants

### 1.2 Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis and Malaria
The Global Fund is the world's largest multilateral funder of global health grants to LMICs, having disbursed over US$65 billion since 2002. While the Global Fund primarily grants to country coordinating mechanisms rather than individual researchers, KHRC can partner on country grants, operational research, and program evaluation for HIV/TB/malaria interventions in Ghana.
- **Research areas covered:** Malaria, TB, HIV, operational/implementation research, health systems.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — exclusively LMIC-focused.
- **URL:** https://www.theglobalfund.org/en/

### 1.3 Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance
Gavi supports vaccine introduction and immunisation research in 41 African countries, including Ghana, which is among the 24 African countries introducing malaria vaccines with Gavi support. Gavi also offers Civil Society Organisation grants to reach zero-dose children and operates the African Vaccine Manufacturing Accelerator (AVMA), a US$1.2 billion facility.
- **Research areas covered:** Vaccines, immunisation operational research, maternal/child health.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — primary geography is sub-Saharan Africa.
- **URL:** https://www.gavi.org/

### 1.4 World Health Organization – TDR (Special Programme for Research and Training in Tropical Diseases)
TDR runs several small-grants and impact-grants schemes explicitly for researchers in developing countries. The **TDR Impact Grants Scheme** and the **Joint WHO-AFRO/TDR Impact Grants** fund implementation research on infectious diseases of poverty, with grants typically US$10,000–US$15,000 per project. There have also been joint calls with EDCTP (Joint WHO-AFRO/TDR/EDCTP Small Grants Scheme) for implementation research on infectious diseases.
- **Research areas covered:** Malaria, NTDs, infectious diseases of poverty, antimicrobial resistance, implementation research.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — only LMIC-based researchers are eligible.
- **URL:** https://tdr.who.int/home/our-work/global-engagement/tdr-small-research-grants-scheme

### 1.5 Children's Investment Fund Foundation (CIFF)
CIFF is one of the world's largest philanthropies focused on children, with a dedicated Africa portfolio and offices in Nairobi and Addis Ababa. Its grants focus on maternal/child health, nutrition (including anaemia), WASH, neglected tropical diseases, adolescent sexual and reproductive health, and climate. CIFF co-founded the Beginnings Fund in 2024 with ELMA Philanthropies, Gates and Delta Philanthropies to reduce maternal and newborn mortality in nine high-burden African countries.
- **Research areas covered:** Maternal, neonatal and child health, nutrition/anaemia, WASH, NTDs, adolescent SRHR, climate.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — the largest share of grants goes to sub-Saharan Africa.
- **URL:** https://ciff.org/

### 1.6 Rockefeller Foundation / Clean Cooking Alliance / Global Energy Alliance for People and Planet
In February 2026, The Rockefeller Foundation, GEAPP, the Clean Cooking Alliance (CCA) and Energy Corps launched the **Clean Cooking Accelerator Initiative** to expand access to modern cooking technologies across Africa. The Clean Cooking Alliance maintains a dedicated funding-opportunities hub for research and capacity-building grants on household air pollution and clean cooking.
- **Research areas covered:** Environmental health (indoor air pollution), clean energy, climate and health.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — most initiatives target sub-Saharan Africa.
- **URL:** https://cleancooking.org/funding-opportunities/

### 1.7 Unitaid
Unitaid invests in innovations to prevent, diagnose and treat HIV, TB, malaria, and women's and children's health conditions in LMICs, including co-funding the Malaria Vaccine Implementation Programme in Ghana, Kenya and Malawi.
- **Research areas covered:** HIV, TB, malaria, maternal/child health, diagnostics, clinical trials.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes.
- **URL:** https://unitaid.org/

### 1.8 International Development Research Centre (IDRC, Canada)
IDRC funds action-oriented research in LMICs and has a large Africa portfolio. Major mechanisms include the **Addressing Neglected Areas of Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights in Sub-Saharan Africa (ANeSA)** initiative (CAD 29.9 million co-funded by IDRC, CIHR and Global Affairs Canada, open to sub-Saharan African lead investigators); the **Innovating for Maternal and Child Health in Africa (IMCHA)** initiative; One Health; and climate-change-and-health regional hubs.
- **Research areas covered:** Maternal & child health, SRHR, One Health, climate and health, implementation research, health policy.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — several SSA-only calls; PIs must be African.
- **URL:** https://idrc-crdi.ca/en/funding

### 1.9 Canadian Institutes of Health Research (CIHR)
CIHR partners with IDRC and Global Affairs Canada on several Africa-facing initiatives (ANeSA, Women RISE, IMCHA, HeLTI). From 2000 to 2019, CIHR invested over CAD 450 million in global health research.
- **Research areas covered:** Global health, maternal/child health, SRHR, NCDs.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes via partnered initiatives.
- **URL:** https://cihr-irsc.gc.ca/e/193.html

## 2. US Government Funders

### 2.1 NIH Fogarty International Center
Fogarty is the principal NIH unit for building sustainable global health research capacity in LMICs. Flagship programs open to Ghana include the **Emerging Global Leader Award (K43)** for LMIC-based junior faculty; **HIV Research Training**; **Global Brain and Nervous System Disorders Research**; the **Global Health Fellows and Scholars/LAUNCH** program; **International Research Scientist Development Award (IRSDA)**; and the **African Postdoctoral Training Initiative (APTI)**. Ghanaian grantees have been supported in areas including sickle cell and malaria, yellow-fever outbreak research, and maternal health.
- **Research areas covered:** All biomedical and behavioral sciences; HIV, malaria, NCDs, maternal health, bioethics, data science.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — Fogarty grants are specifically designed for LMIC capacity building.
- **URL:** https://www.fic.nih.gov/Funding

### 2.2 NIH (Parent Institutes: NIAID, NICHD, NIEHS, NHLBI, Fogarty, NHGRI)
Beyond Fogarty, multiple NIH Institutes accept applications from foreign institutions and/or U.S. consortia with African partners. Examples include the **Data Science for Health Discovery and Innovation in Africa (DS-I Africa)** consortium (38 projects by 2023); **Human Heredity and Health in Africa (H3Africa)**; and NIAID-funded HIV/TB research networks.
- **Research areas covered:** Genomics, HIV/AIDS, TB, malaria, NCDs, data science, environmental health.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — H3Africa, DS-I Africa and numerous LMIC programs.
- **URL:** https://grants.nih.gov/funding/searchguide/index.html

### 2.3 US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
CDC funds research, surveillance and capacity-building through cooperative agreements on malaria (President's Malaria Initiative, PMI), HIV (PEPFAR), and global health security. Most CDC awards are competed on Grants.gov and require US-based prime partners but commonly sub-award to Ghanaian institutions.
- **Research areas covered:** Malaria, HIV, TB, vaccines, global health security, maternal/child health.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes, implementation in SSA; prime awardees are typically US-based.
- **URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/grants/

### 2.4 USAID (status cautionary – 2025 retrenchment)
Historically USAID has been the largest bilateral funder of Ghana's health sector, funding maternal/newborn/child health, malaria, family planning and WASH through mechanisms such as the **Ghana Health Program (GHP)**. However, the January 2025 USAID funding freeze cancelled approximately 90% of USAID's global projects, significantly reducing this pipeline. KHRC researchers should monitor USAID Ghana and grants.gov for residual/post-restructuring funding opportunities.
- **Research areas covered:** Maternal/child health, malaria, family planning, HIV, WASH, nutrition.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes (when active).
- **URL:** https://www.usaid.gov/ghana

### 2.5 PEPFAR (U.S. President's Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief)
PEPFAR funds HIV/AIDS research and service implementation across Africa, often with operational research components delivered through CDC and USAID mechanisms.
- **Research areas covered:** HIV prevention, treatment, operational research, co-infections (TB, cervical cancer).
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes, exclusively LMIC.
- **URL:** https://www.state.gov/pepfar/

### 2.6 President's Malaria Initiative (PMI)
PMI, jointly implemented by USAID and CDC, funds malaria control programs and operational/implementation research in Ghana and across 27 African partner countries, although this too is affected by the 2025 USAID funding changes.
- **Research areas covered:** Malaria (case management, vector control, surveillance, drug/insecticide resistance), implementation research.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes.
- **URL:** https://www.pmi.gov/

### 2.7 NIH National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences (NIEHS) / Clean Cooking Research
NIEHS supports household and ambient air pollution research and has jointly sponsored workshops on indoor air pollution affecting women and children in developing countries. It co-funded the HAPIN (Household Air Pollution Intervention Network) trial and supports clean-cooking research.
- **Research areas covered:** Environmental health, air pollution, climate and health.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Strong LMIC component.
- **URL:** https://www.niehs.nih.gov/funding

## 3. European Funders

### 3.1 Global Health EDCTP3 Joint Undertaking (European Commission + EDCTP Association)
EDCTP3 is the single most important Africa-Europe clinical-research funding partnership. The **2026 Work Programme** makes €147 million available for TB, HIV, lower respiratory tract infections, climate and health, and capacity building, with all calls requiring at least one sub-Saharan African partner. Across 107 Global Health EDCTP3-funded projects (total €430 million), 49% of participants are based in Africa. Specific 2026 calls relevant to KHRC include:
- HORIZON-JU-GH-EDCTP3-2026-01-TB-01: TB drug development in adults & children in SSA.
- HORIZON-JU-GH-EDCTP3-2026-01-LRTI-02: Lower respiratory tract infections in SSA.
- HORIZON-JU-GH-EDCTP3-2026-01-HIV-03: HIV co-infections/co-morbidities in SSA.
- HORIZON-JU-GH-EDCTP3-2026-02-CH-01: Climate and health in SSA.
- HORIZON-JU-GH-EDCTP3-2026-03-SERP-01: Ethics/regulatory/pharmacovigilance training networks.
- HORIZON-JU-GH-EDCTP3-2026-03-DIGIT-02: Digital innovation & AI in SSA.
- **Research areas covered:** Clinical trials; malaria & NTDs; HIV; TB; climate & health; vaccines; implementation research.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — mandatory SSA partnership; programme explicitly builds African research capacity.
- **URL:** https://www.global-health-edctp3.europa.eu/

### 3.2 Wellcome Trust
Wellcome Trust is one of the largest independent health research charities in the world. Researchers in sub-Saharan Africa are eligible to apply within Wellcome's broad Science funding remit. Key schemes:
- **Climate Impacts Awards** – up to £2.5 million per award for transdisciplinary teams investigating climate-health links, with Wellcome having already awarded £22.7m including projects in Ghana.
- **Climate and Health Award: Advancing climate mitigation solutions with health co-benefits in LMICs** – LMIC-led transdisciplinary teams.
- **Wellcome Career Development Awards** – mid-career researchers, LMIC-based eligible.
- **Discovery Awards, Early-Career Awards, Seed Awards in Science**, and Wellcome funding for infectious disease, mental health, and climate & health.
- **Research areas covered:** Infectious disease (incl. malaria, NTDs), climate & health, mental health, environmental health, clinical trials.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — explicit LMIC-led funding preference; SSA researchers fully eligible across science remit.
- **URL:** https://wellcome.org/grant-funding

### 3.3 UK National Institute for Health and Care Research (NIHR) – Global Health Research Portfolio
From 2026, NIHR prioritises research in sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia, offering some of the most substantial grants open to African-led partnerships. Ghana is ODA-eligible and therefore qualifies. Programmes include:
- **Global Health Research – Researcher-led** (3 funding bands, both LMIC- or UK-led lead applicants).
- **Global Health Research (GHR) Themed Programme** – £0.5m to £5m over 3–5 years for global health security challenges.
- **Global Advanced Fellowships** – up to £750,000 for postdoctoral researchers based in ODA-eligible LMICs including SSA. The Liverpool-led **CLEAN-Air(Africa)** Global Health Research Unit has been an NIHR showcase project on household air pollution, with KHRC as a named partner.
- **Research areas covered:** All applied global health – maternal/newborn/child/adolescent health, infectious diseases, health systems, environmental health, clinical trials.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — strategic priority from 2026 for SSA and South Asia.
- **URL:** https://www.nihr.ac.uk/research-funding/global-health

### 3.4 UK Medical Research Council (UKRI-MRC)
The MRC co-funds EDCTP programs and operates bilateral research grants often open to African-led proposals in infectious diseases, maternal/child health, and clinical trials.
- **Research areas covered:** Clinical trials, infectious diseases, biomedical sciences.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes, via Global Challenges Research Fund legacy and MRC/DFID partnerships.
- **URL:** https://www.ukri.org/councils/mrc/

### 3.5 Horizon Europe – Cluster 1 "Health" (European Commission)
Horizon Europe Cluster 1 opens 18 topics in 2026 on health, including topics of direct relevance to KHRC: climate change and health (HORIZON-HLTH-2026-01-ENVHLTH-01), climate-resilient healthcare systems (ENVHLTH-04), multisectoral approaches to NCDs (DISEASE-09, GACD-aligned implementation research), post-infection long-term conditions, and vaccines for viral pathogens. Most projects require multi-country consortia; African associated countries can participate.
- **Research areas covered:** Environmental health & climate, NCDs, vaccines, implementation research, health systems.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Partial — consortia with African partners are encouraged; not Africa-exclusive.
- **URL:** https://research-and-innovation.ec.europa.eu/funding/funding-opportunities/funding-programmes-and-open-calls/horizon-europe/cluster-1-health_en

### 3.6 Innovative Health Initiative (IHI) – EU Public-Private Partnership
IHI runs regular calls on integrated healthcare and digital health, and permits international partners.
- **Research areas covered:** Clinical trials, digital health, integrated healthcare.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Limited but possible through consortia.
- **URL:** https://www.ihi.europa.eu/

### 3.7 Swedish International Development Cooperation Agency (Sida)
Sida co-funds EDCTP through its membership, and independently supports African research capacity through bilateral programs and the Science Granting Councils Initiative.
- **Research areas covered:** Infectious diseases, SRHR, health systems.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes.
- **URL:** https://www.sida.se/en

### 3.8 German Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF)
BMBF co-finances EDCTP and Grand Challenges Africa Maternal Neonatal & Child Health calls in partnership with AAS and the Gates Foundation.
- **Research areas covered:** MNCH, NTDs, infectious disease research capacity in Africa.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes via partnered channels.
- **URL:** https://www.bmbf.de/EN

### 3.9 UK Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO)
FCDO co-funds DELTAS Africa II and the Science Granting Councils Initiative in Africa, and provides ODA backing to NIHR Global Health Research and IDRC partnerships.
- **Research areas covered:** Research capacity, infectious diseases, climate and health.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes.
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/international-development-funding

## 4. African Regional Funders

### 4.1 Science for Africa Foundation (SFA Foundation)
The SFA Foundation is a pan-African, non-profit public charity that has committed to mobilising and spending US$250 million by 2027 to directly support African scientists. It now manages several of the largest African-led programmes:
- **DELTAS Africa II (2023–2026):** 14 research consortia across 57 African institutions, funded by Wellcome and FCDO, covering malaria, HIV/TB, genomics, mental health, One Health and public health.
- **Grand Challenges Africa (GC Africa):** Innovation seed, transition, and scaling grants for African investigators in MNCH, climate and health, and gender.
- **Research areas covered:** Infectious diseases (malaria, HIV, TB), maternal/child health, climate & environment, genomics, mental health, One Health.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — 100% African-led.
- **URL:** https://scienceforafrica.foundation/funding

### 4.2 African Academy of Sciences (AAS) / AESA
Although several AESA-managed programmes (Grand Challenges, DELTAS) have transitioned to the SFA Foundation, AAS continues to operate funding schemes of direct relevance to KHRC researchers, including the **African Research Initiative for Scientific Excellence (ARISE)** – co-funded by the European Union and Carnegie Corporation, with grants up to €500,000 over five years for African PIs; **AESA-RISE Postdoctoral Fellowships**; **Africa-India Mobility Fund**; **Research Management Programme in Africa (ReMPro Africa)**; and the **Science Communication/Africa Science Desk** programme.
- **Research areas covered:** Health, STEM, research capacity, policy — including MNCH and infectious diseases.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — exclusively for African-based researchers.
- **URL:** https://aasciences.africa/funding-opportunities

### 4.3 African Union / AUDA-NEPAD
The African Union Development Agency (AUDA-NEPAD) historically partnered with AAS/Gates/DFID to launch Grand Challenges Africa. AUDA-NEPAD continues to offer targeted calls for proposals via its tenders portal on research and innovation in health, climate and STI.
- **Research areas covered:** Health systems, maternal health, STI, One Health, policy evaluation.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — continental mandate.
- **URL:** https://www.nepad.org/

### 4.4 Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention (Africa CDC)
Africa CDC, the autonomous public health body of the African Union, channels funding into member-state capacity, surveillance, outbreak research, vaccine manufacturing and pandemic preparedness. A US$100 million IDA-financed World Bank project approved in 2022 boosted Africa CDC's technical and research capacity. Africa CDC also partners on pandemic-preparedness calls and supports regional research programs in epidemic preparedness.
- **Research areas covered:** Infectious diseases, epidemics, health policy, vaccine R&D, surveillance, pandemic preparedness.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — African-led.
- **URL:** https://africacdc.org/

### 4.5 African Development Bank (AfDB)
AfDB funds clean cooking, maternal health, nutrition and health-systems strengthening across Africa, increasingly including research components. The Bank has pledged US$2 billion over ten years towards clean cooking solutions in Africa and is a co-lead of Mission 300.
- **Research areas covered:** Environmental health/clean cooking, nutrition, maternal/child health, climate.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes.
- **URL:** https://www.afdb.org/en

### 4.6 Coalition for Operational Research on Neglected Tropical Diseases (COR-NTD) – African Researchers' Small Grants Program
The COR-NTD African Researchers' Small Grants Program (SGP) has provided more than US$1.5 million to over 66 researchers from 16 African countries for NTD implementation research. SGP VII was announced in 2024; current status post-USAID restructuring should be confirmed directly.
- **Research areas covered:** NTDs, implementation research.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — explicitly for African researchers.
- **URL:** https://www.cor-ntd.org/SGP

### 4.7 Africa Health Collaborative
The Africa Health Collaborative Research Fund supports Early Career Researchers from member institutions — including Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (Ghana) — for bilateral/multilateral primary-healthcare research. The 2025 call closed in April 2025; a 2026 cycle is anticipated.
- **Research areas covered:** Primary healthcare, community/public health, health systems.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes.
- **URL:** https://africahealthcollaborative.org/research/research-fund/

### 4.8 Science Granting Councils Initiative (SGCI) in Sub-Saharan Africa
A multi-funder initiative (IDRC, Sida, Norad, FCDO, NRF South Africa, DFG) that has supported 17 councils across SSA over a decade. Ghana's science council is a member, offering grants and capacity-building.
- **Research areas covered:** All STI; health research through national councils.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes.
- **URL:** https://sgciafrica.org/

### 4.9 Tony Elumelu Foundation (TEF)
While TEF is primarily an entrepreneurship funder (US$5,000 seed grants) and explicitly does not fund research institutions, it has funded over 700 healthcare entrepreneurs across Africa and is relevant for KHRC researchers considering health-innovation spin-offs.
- **Research areas covered:** Health entrepreneurship (not academic research).
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes — 54 African countries.
- **URL:** https://www.tonyelumelufoundation.org/

### 4.10 African Population and Health Research Center (APHRC) / Africa-led consortia
APHRC and similar Africa-led research organisations (e.g. KEMRI-Wellcome, WACCBIP at the University of Ghana) regularly lead or sub-grant DELTAS, Wellcome, NIH and Gates-funded consortia in which KHRC can participate as a spoke institution.
- **Research areas covered:** MNCH, climate and health, urban health, data science.
- **Africa/LMIC focus:** Yes.
- **URL:** https://aphrc.org/

## 6. Strategic Recommendations for KHRC

1. **Prioritise EDCTP3 and NIHR Global Health Research**: Both are stable, large, Africa-led or equitable-partnership funders with 2026 calls directly matching KHRC's entire research portfolio, including climate-and-health and implementation research.
2. **Leverage SFA Foundation (DELTAS Africa II, Grand Challenges Africa) and AAS (ARISE)**: These are African-led mechanisms where KHRC can lead proposals rather than participate as a junior sub-awardee.
3. **Use Wellcome's LMIC-led Climate & Health and Career Development Awards** for environmental-health research, complementing EDCTP3's climate-and-health topic.
4. **Maintain NIH Fogarty engagement** through Emerging Global Leader (K43), APTI, and LAUNCH for individual capacity-building, recognising that broader NIH/USAID/CDC funding to Ghana is being restructured in 2025–26.
5. **Monitor TDR, COR-NTD and WHO/AFRO small-grants schemes** for rapid-turnaround implementation research funding on malaria, NTDs and AMR — smaller amounts (US$10k–US$150k) but accessible directly to Ghanaian PIs.
6. **Build consortium memberships** with APHRC, KEMRI-Wellcome, WACCBIP (University of Ghana), and the NIHR CLEAN-Air(Africa) Unit to become eligible for multi-country funding on climate, nutrition/anaemia and air pollution.
7. **Watch for revived US mechanisms and pivot to non-traditional donors** (Qatar/Saudi Arabia-backed Islamic Development Bank Lives and Livelihoods Fund, Chinese health cooperation, CIFF, Beginnings Fund) as the post-USAID global health financing landscape evolves.
